We begin with zero because, of course, counting starts at zero. Like celebrating my newborn daughter's zeroeth birthday. (see photo at bottom of post)


Day 0 began as one might expect addressing administrative and logistical issues. Then moved into a discussion of programming.


Our instructor, Clinton, then gave a mind blowing example of hacking a binary sort algorithm into a ternary sort by taking advantage of the fact that a double beam balance doesn't just produce an output of heavier or lighter but heavier, lighter, or equal. It took a while to wrap my brain around it but very cool stuff.


We had a bonus lecture from John Saddington, serial entrepreneur and one of the Iron Yard partners. He gave a lot of honest answers about entrepreneurship. This is the kind of thing in which one rarely gets to participate outside a class like this or small conference.


The last part of classroom time was dedicated to software installation. The kind of stuff that drives me insane at home because, of course, I installed some weird thing ages ago that makes the instructions not work as expected on my machine. Very nice to have help and get any issues resolved quickly.


Then came the homework. Blackjack 21. I have never played blackjack. I though I understood it. Player and dealer get two cards. Player optionally selects a third card. Whomever gets closes to 21 with out going over wins. Then I read the rules. I was very wrong. It is far more complex than I believed. Couldn't we do "go fish" for our first project? Off to try to understand blackjack. More tomorrow...
