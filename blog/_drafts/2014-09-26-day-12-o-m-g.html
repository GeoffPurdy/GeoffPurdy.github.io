I have noticed that my written communication with other humans is beginning to look a lot like Ruby.  If I get more concerned then I will make changes else I will change nothing.

We had a visitor in the class today.  He is kind of short and much younger than the rest of the class but seems extremely friendly and likable.

What a difference a day makes.  I finished the weekend lab due next Mon last night (Thurs).  What to do between now and Monday morning?  I could exercise (if I can remember what that is). I could spend time with my family (I have already been doing that).  I could do some things around the house that had been out-prioritized like changing lightbulbs and mowing the grass.

Or, I could redo the weekend assignment in another language like Clojure.  That would be cool.  I'll see if any of my fellow students need help and if they don't then Clojure it will be.  I should also go back and finish the ruby koans that I didn't complete earlier in the week.  I am not looking forward to that.

The weekend assignment was our choice of several games the most difficult of which, I was warned, was Conway's Game of Life.  Life is something I had been meaning to write for years so I jumped at the chance.  I found TDD to be very helpful.  The TDD methodology allowed me to write fairly extensive tests around boundary and edge cases for translating 1D arrays to 2D and back.  This is the kind of thing that historically would have driven me crazy with off-by-one errors.  But once I had the tests running, I didn't encounter such errors at all.  I would guess TDD reduced development time by at least 50% for me on this project.
