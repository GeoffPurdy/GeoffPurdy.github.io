Show me.

Was it really hard, or you just couldn't do the work?

Neither.  The classroom instruction model just hasn't worked that well for me.  My original thinking was that because it cost $10,000 there must be some sort of "special sauce" that accelerates learning and makes it less frustrating or painful.  Not an "upload" a la The Matrix but something between that and what one can accomplish independently.

For me, the experience inside this program looks virtually identical to learning outside the program.  I found I relied heavily on outside resources like books and online training courses to get the work done.  Long periods of frustration followed by flashes of insight.  Rinse, repeat.

At $10,000 per seat, there should be a very strong value proposition versus the next best alternative.  Twelve weeks of "all you can eat" e-learning at Infinite Skills costs a whopping $54.  $10,000 vs $54  I am just not seeing nearly that much added value in the classroom training.  To be honest, I prefer the online training regardless of cost.


What will you do instead?

Catch up on Springer?  Judge Judy?  Um, no.  I have become hooked on online education platforms (Coursera, edX, Udacity, Udemy) where on can rewind lectures and re-watch them as much as you want.  I can pause the lecture and google a concept I don't understand then return to the lecture without missing any content.  Having gone back to a classroom lecture model, I realized how much I missed these features and had come to rely on them.  The fact that classroom learning is two to three orders of magnitude more expensive also factors heavily into the equation.  Despite being in a classroom course, I had turned to online courseware as a supplement and found it is more my style than the classroom lectures.


Are you giving up on Rails / Web Development / Software Engineering?

Of course not.  I am already well underway in online training to complete Rails and move on to web development using the MEAN stack.



Do you think poorly of The Iron Yard?

No.  The Iron Yard model works really well for some.  I have personally witnessed people go from non-programmer to creating websites in a surprisingly short time.  

Unfortunately, the model hasn't worked particularly well for me compared to online learning platforms.
