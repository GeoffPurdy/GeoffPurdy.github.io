No instructors or TA's on site today so students have the run of the space.  Most of the class trickled in over the course of the morning.  This weekend's assignment is challenging for me because of a) being new to Ruby and b) BlackJack has a fairly complex set of rules and strategies.  Challenging but managable.

I feel for the students who are brand new to programming.  Getting hit with programming, Ruby,  git, OOP, and OO analysis all in the course of a week has to be traumatic.  I suspect that wrestling with the problem domain is preparing the students to see the value in tools we will cover in coming weeks:  OOP, unit testing, debugging tools, modules, and libraries / frameworks.  Hopefully nobody is freaking out.

My good intentioned attempts to help other students may be backfiring.  It occurred to me that most students don't know OOP so me talking about creating objects and deciding how they will interact may have just added to the confusion.
