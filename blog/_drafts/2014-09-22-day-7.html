--- layout: post title: 'Day 7: In the driver''s seat' date: '2014-09-22T06:03:00.000-07:00' author: geoff purdy tags: modified_time: '2017-03-26T05:00:52.524-07:00' thumbnail: http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-DnEigm-1eC0/VCAu87DjjPI/AAAAAAAABlw/g2c6jsoUHKc/s72-c/class.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1206336038417661147.post-5360272383089005285 blogger_orig_url: http://geoffreypurdy.blogspot.com/2014/09/day-7.html --- I have to say I am completely in love with programming again!  I probably spent 60 hours coding this past week and I enjoyed every minute of it.

There were times a few years ago while sitting in endless conference calls with faceless names on other continents that I really believed the IT industry wasn't for me.

There have been times in the recent past while taking MOOC's from Stanford, MIT, and the like that I had become convinced that I just didn't have the intellectual horsepower to keep up in this field.

There were times while studying on my own that I seriously believed that I had ADD or a learning disability of some sort preventing me from absorbing knowledge as quickly as I needed to.


But I love the format of this class.  There is expert help available pretty much 24/7 so annoying problems don't become roadblocks.  And best of all there are a bunch of cool people in the same boat and in physical proximity whom you can support and look to for support.  For the first time in a decade or more, I feel confident and in the driver's seat of my career.
