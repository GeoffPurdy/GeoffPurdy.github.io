I hate ruby koans.  Yesterday morning I had never heard of koans, now I hate them with an all consuming passion.  I have worked on these for the last 24 hours and completed 41.  There are 282 total and we are expected to complete all of them by tonight.

The tl;dr version of ruby koans is that you are given a ruby program that won't run because there are 1000+ intentional errors in the code.  The student fixes them one at a time and attempts to build the codebase to get to the next error/koan.  What separates koans from a badly broken program is that the error messages are wrapped with Zen-like witticisms like "The path to enlightenment requires the defenestration of one's laptop."  Okay, I made that one up but they are similar.

I would so much like to spend this time reading The Ruby Programming Language instead of slogging through these koans.  I think I may bag the koans and do exactly that.  Did I mention how I feel about koans?
