--- layout: post title: 'Day 20: Meditations on HTML and CSS' date: '2014-10-04T11:10:00.003-07:00' author: geoff purdy tags: modified_time: '2017-03-26T05:00:52.517-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1206336038417661147.post-5187721912608943290 blogger_orig_url: http://geoffreypurdy.blogspot.com/2014/10/day-20-meditations-on-html-and-css.html --- I have been reading hundreds of different flavors of CSS Zen Garden and am feeling contemplative.

At one point I was under the impression that HTML contained content and CSS contained style and never the two should meet.

Thus inlining one's CSS was Very Bad®:

<h1 style="color:blue;margin-left:20px;">My heading.</h1>

Because in the example above one's style is intermingled with one's content.

The Answer®:  Break the style into a separate .css file:

in the .html
<link rel="stylesheet" type="text/css" href="style.css"><div class="my-first-heading-in-the-second-column"><h1>This is a heading.</h1></div>

in the .css
.my-first-heading-in-the-second-column h1 {    color: blue;    margin-left: 20px;}

Thus far this week my brain has rejected this as The Answer® because style is still interspersed with the content just in a different form and some of the specifics are offloaded to another file.

After meditating on CSS Zen Garden, I think I am finally beginning to wrap my brain around this.

<div class="benefits id="zen-benefits" role="article"><h3>Benefits</h3>  <p>Why participate? For recognition, inspiration...</p></div>

The Zen Garden markup above is describing the content rather than describing the presentation style.  I think that is a subtle but important distinction.

But I am still not happy with Zen Garden.  My thinking is that the markup structure of the HTML document should reflect the semantic structure of the content rather than the parroting or summarizing the content itself.

This would make sense for an essay
<div class="essay">
  <div class="thesis"></div>
  <div class="support"></div>
  <div class="conclusion"></div>
</div>
or for news
<div class="news">   <div class="headline"></div>   <div class="dek"></div>   <div class="billboard"></div>   <div class="subhead"></div>   <div class="graf"></div>   <div class="kicker"></div></div>

Of course, not all human generated content is as rigidly structured as these examples.  Perhaps someday I will figure this out.
