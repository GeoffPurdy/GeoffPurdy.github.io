On a positive note, we are moving into some new territory for me - unit testing.  I recall using the Turing language at UNC in the mid-90's.  Turing allowed one to specify preconditions and postconditions when entering and exiting functions.  The professor told us this was going to be a Big Deal in the Future but I really did't encounter the concept again until unit tests in Extreme Programming around 2000.

On another positive note, I like unit tests.  And TDD worked very well for this project.  I had done a 2 day TDD workshop a few years ago and I found the resulting code to be excessively verbose, inefficient, and poorly thought out compared to a top down design.  In the case of something very simple and mathematical like CurrencyConverter , TDD gave a very clean design and implementation.
